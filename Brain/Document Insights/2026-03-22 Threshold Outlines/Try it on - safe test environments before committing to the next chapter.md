## Core Insight

A specific methodology in the Renewal and Transition work: creating safe test environments for the leader to experience a possible next chapter before committing to it. The purpose is to make the next step real rather than theoretical. Examples include volunteering, board roles, and a month at a vineyard. This is experiential discernment - not planning, not analysis, but embodied exploration.

---

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - a named methodology within Luminous practice; consistent with design thinking principles (prototyping and iteration) applied to identity and career transitions

**What makes this distinct from job exploration**:

Standard career transition advice directs people toward networking, informational interviews, and job applications. The "try it on" methodology operates at a different level. The goal is not to gather information about an industry or role. It is to discover how it *feels* to inhabit a different way of being in the world. Volunteering is not about building a CV entry - it is about what wakes up in the leader when they are doing it.

**Examples named in the document**:
- Volunteering - exposure to non-commercial purpose, different social context, different measures of success
- Board roles - governance and advisory mode, different relationship to leadership than operational executive work
- "A month at a vineyard" - the most vivid example: immersive, embodied, completely outside the corporate frame

**Why this matters**: Leaders at this threshold often get stuck in analysis. They can generate lists of options, research industries, and reason about trade-offs indefinitely. The impasse is not cognitive - it is experiential. They do not know how it will feel. The "try it on" methodology bypasses the analytical loop by providing direct experience. The body often knows before the mind does.

**Sequencing in the coaching arc**: This methodology appears as the final step in the coaching outline - after emotional processing, identity clarification (values, strengths, purpose), financial reckoning, and community mapping. It is not introduced before the inner work is done. Otherwise, the experimentation simply adds more noise to an already confused search.
