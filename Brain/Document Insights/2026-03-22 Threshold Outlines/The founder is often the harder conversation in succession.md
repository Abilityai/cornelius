# The Founder Is Often the Harder Conversation in Succession

## Core Framework

In family business succession, the presenting challenge is often framed around the next-generation leader - their readiness, identity, and capability. But the more difficult coaching conversation is typically with the founding generation. Next-gen leaders are generally hungry to grow and open to new thinking. Founders are not always willing to genuinely release - and their willingness, or lack of it, shapes everything that follows.

This is a counterintuitive reframe: the apparent "problem holder" (the heir) is rarely where the real work is stuck. The bottleneck is upstream.

---

## Why This Matters

When succession support is directed primarily at the next-gen leader, it can leave the actual source of blockage untouched. A fully developed heir who has done deep inner work is still unable to lead if the founder continues to make decisions from the sidelines, override choices informally, or maintain the expectation of deference. Founder willingness is a prerequisite condition, not an optional add-on.

---

## Practical Implication

Any succession engagement must assess the founding generation's readiness to genuinely release - not merely step back in name. This is often the first diagnostic question: is the founder willing, or are we working around an immovable constraint?
