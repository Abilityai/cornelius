# The 80-20 rule of inner work at the Scale threshold

## Core Framework

A distinctive coaching insight specific to the Scale threshold: unlike leaders at the Career Transition or Long-Term Impact thresholds, founders and operators at Scale are often earlier in their developmental journey. Their readiness for inner work varies significantly - and pushing too hard too fast loses them.

The practical principle: the ratio of outer to inner work may start at 80/20 and shift gradually over the course of the engagement. Begin with operational, structural, and role-design conversations. Introduce deeper reflection only as trust and readiness develop.

---

## Context and Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging practice wisdom within Luminous coaching; consistent with adult development research on meeting people at their current developmental level
**Related Principles**: Kegan's notion of "the curriculum has to match the developmental stage"; the counselling principle of pacing
**Alternative Risk**: Jumping too quickly to identity and inner work with a founder at Scale can trigger defensive retreat - they may disengage, dismiss the process as "soft", or simply not return

---

## Why This Threshold Is Different

The Luminous framework distinguishes between thresholds by the degree of interior readiness they typically arrive with:

- **Career Transition (Metanoia)** and **Long-Term Impact (Dharma)**: Leaders at these thresholds have usually been asking deeper questions for some time. They are more likely to arrive open to inner work, to welcome reflection, and to be seeking something they cannot fully name.

- **Scale (Kairos)**: Founders and operators at this threshold are often still primarily in "solve the problem" mode. They are resourceful, action-oriented, and may have little prior exposure to inner work. They come for business help. The deeper work opens from there, gradually, as the relationship deepens and as the outer-work framings begin to reveal their limits.

The 80/20 starting ratio is not a permanent ceiling - it is a description of where many founders begin and an instruction not to rush past it. The coaching work at Scale often involves building enough trust and demonstrating enough practical value that the leader becomes willing to look inward. That willingness cannot be installed; it can only be earned.

---

## Practical Implication

The entry points at this threshold are deliberately operational:
- Role redesign conversations
- Interview-based feedback from the executive team
- Structural authority questions

These are not the whole story, but they are the right place to start. They create credibility, surface the patterns, and - when handled well - create the openings through which deeper work can enter. Purpose and legacy questions (see: [[Purpose as a coaching entry point at Scale]]) serve a similar bridging function.
