## Core Framework

When purpose work moves from individual to team, a critical reframe unlocks the conversation: the shift from inside-out to outside-in.

Inside-out: *"What do we want to be the best at?"*
Outside-in: *"What is the world asking for from us?"*

The first question produces competitive positioning. It is energising in early stages of a business and exhausting in later ones - because no one is truly inspired by being the best at a category. The second question produces mission. It connects the team's work to something larger than the organisation, and that connection is intrinsically motivating in a way that competitive aspiration is not.

The reframe changes the conversation entirely. It shifts the leadership team from a discussion about internal capability to a discussion about external contribution - from *what can we do?* to *what are we for?*

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - consistent with Purpose-Driven Organisation literature (Mackey & Sisodia, Bartlett & Ghoshal), Simon Sinek's "Start With Why," and decades of positive organisational psychology. The specific outside-in framing and the observation that it "changes the conversation entirely" is Luminous's practitioner insight.

**Supporting Evidence**: Research consistently shows that purpose-driven teams outperform on engagement and retention (Deloitte, McKinsey purpose research). The mechanism aligns with self-determination theory (Deci & Ryan) - autonomous motivation increases when work connects to something beyond the self.

**Important Caveat**: This is a practitioner framework drawn from coaching experience; the specific claim about its transformative effect is observational, not empirical.

---

## The Mechanism

The outside-in question:
- Depersonalises the conversation (removes ego competition between team members about whose vision wins)
- Introduces the customer/world as a third party the team serves together
- Makes the answer emergent rather than mandated by the leader
- Creates accountability to something larger than the team itself

The GSK Kenya example from the source document illustrates the most powerful version: leaders encountering the end impact of their work directly - meeting the people their products actually serve. This is outside-in made embodied.
