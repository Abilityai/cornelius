## Core Framework

The Luminous approach to coaching leaders at the Scale threshold explicitly names the Immunity to Change (ITC) methodology as a core tool. The application: help the leader name what they know they need to do differently, then excavate the hidden commitments and assumptions that keep them doing the opposite.

The ITC framework (developed by Robert Kegan and Lisa Laskow Lahey at Harvard) is particularly well-suited to this threshold because the Scale challenge is almost always an adaptive problem - one where the obstacle is not lack of skill or information, but the presence of competing commitments the leader is not yet aware of.

## The Four-Column Structure Applied at Scale

The ITC process surfaces what Kegan and Lahey call the "immune system" - the hidden architecture of commitments that counteract the leader's stated intentions.

At the Scale threshold, the map typically looks like this:

**Column 1 - Improvement Goal**: "I want to genuinely delegate decision-making authority to my leadership team."

**Column 2 - What I do instead** (undermining behaviours): "I micromanage decisions after delegating them. I override team choices without explanation. I hold all-hands meetings where I answer questions that should go to the leadership team."

**Column 3 - Hidden competing commitments**: "I am deeply committed to not losing control of what might go wrong. I am committed to not being held responsible for other people's failures. I am committed to remaining the most competent person in the room."

**Column 4 - Big assumptions**: "If I genuinely give up control, things will go wrong and I will be blamed. If others lead, they will do it differently than I would, and differently means worse. If I am not the expert in the room, I am not needed."

The power of the approach is that it reframes the leader's failure to change as not a character flaw but a structural logic - one that made sense at an earlier stage and now needs to be examined rather than fought.

---

## Why ITC Works Particularly Well at Scale

Leaders at this threshold are often highly intelligent and self-aware - they know what they should be doing. The gap between knowing and doing is not an information problem. ITC provides language and structure for the thing they already sense: that there is something in them that is actively working against what they say they want. That naming - delivered without judgment - is often experienced as a profound relief.
