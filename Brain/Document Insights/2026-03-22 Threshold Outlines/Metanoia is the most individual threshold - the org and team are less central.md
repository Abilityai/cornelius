# Metanoia Is the Most Individual Threshold - the Org and Team Are Less Central

**Source**: Luminous Internal Document - "Renewal and Transition - Threshold Outline"

## Core Insight

Among the Five Thresholds, Metanoia is distinctively individual. The organisational and team dimension - central at Scale, Transformation, and Succession - is less relevant here. The work happens in the interior of the leader, not across systems. This shapes what coaching looks like and what it does not need to include.

---

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Theoretical - an explicit structural observation about the Luminous threshold taxonomy; distinguishes Metanoia from the other four thresholds along the individual-systemic axis

**Why this structural distinction matters**:

The other four thresholds all have a significant system dimension:
- Scale: the organisation is straining under growth; team dynamics, delegation, and structural change are all in play
- Transformation: culture has not caught up with strategy; team and culture work are central
- Succession: the handover involves two people, two generations, often a family system
- Long-Term Impact: often includes legacy structures, philanthropic or institutional commitments, communities affected by the leader's choices

Metanoia is different. The leader's organisation continues. Their team continues. The locus of change is the leader's relationship to their own life. The coaching need is not "how do I lead differently?" but "who am I becoming, and what is my life actually for?"

**Implications for offering design**:
- Team diagnostics and 360s are less relevant
- The cohort model (in development) is about peer support for a shared passage, not team dynamics
- The ripple effect to families and communities is real but indirect - it flows through the leader's becoming, not through direct systemic intervention

**The ripple effect is still significant**: The document notes that "families and communities benefit when leaders make this transition well." The individual nature of the threshold does not diminish its systemic impact - it simply means the mechanism is different. Change at the level of one person's identity radiates outward through all their relationships.
