# Purpose-Legacy-Stewardship - Three Words, One Underlying Shift

## Core Framework

Purpose, legacy, and stewardship are not three separate concerns - they are three entry points to the same underlying shift: from I to we, from ego to contribution, from achievement to responsibility. A leader may arrive at the threshold through any of these doors, but the territory they enter is the same.

- **Purpose** opens with the question: *why am I here?*
- **Legacy** opens with the question: *what is the broader impact I want to have?*
- **Stewardship** opens with the question: *what am I custodian of, for future generations?*

Each question sounds different. Each leads inward, toward the same depth. The distinction in language matters for meeting the leader where they are - but the work that follows is unified.

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - this is a Luminous-specific framing that synthesises developmental psychology and wisdom traditions. The constituent ideas (purpose, legacy, stewardship as developmental moves) have broad support; the triad formulation is Luminous's own.

**Supporting Evidence**: Consistent with ego development theory (Loevinger, Cook-Greuter), where post-conventional stages involve a shift from self-authorship to a sense of service and contribution. Also consistent with logotherapy (Frankl) and meaning research (Steger).

**Related Theories**: Maslow's hierarchy (purpose emerges once security and belonging are established), Erikson's generativity stage, Kegan's subject-object theory.

---

## Why This Framing Matters

The triad prevents a common coaching error: treating purpose, legacy, and stewardship as sequential steps or distinct programmes. A leader who says "I want to think about my legacy" and one who says "I've been wondering about my purpose" are describing the same interior movement - and should receive the same depth of care, not different curricula.

The practical implication: Luminous can enter through any of the three doors and trust that the work is the same once inside.
