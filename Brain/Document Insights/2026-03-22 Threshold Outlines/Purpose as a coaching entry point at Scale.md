# Purpose as a coaching entry point at Scale

## Core Framework

When working with founders and operators at the Scale threshold who are not yet ready for explicit inner work, connecting the leader to long-term vision - "what do you want this to become?" - functions as a less threatening opening for deeper reflection.

Purpose is positioned as a bridging question: it is simultaneously strategic (and therefore acceptable to the action-oriented founder) and existential (and therefore capable of opening something deeper). It does not require the leader to talk about fear or identity directly. It invites them to think forward - about legacy, about what they are building, about what would make this worth all the difficulty - in a way that naturally draws out the questions that matter.

---

## Context and Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Recognised in leadership coaching and positive psychology; consistent with Seligman's PERMA framework and Frankl's logotherapy applied to organisational contexts
**Related Approaches**: Simon Sinek's "Start With Why" operates in adjacent territory; Viktor Frankl's meaning-centred approach; Positive Psychology's emphasis on meaning over pleasure
**Why This Works**: Vision and purpose questions are culturally legitimate in business contexts - no founder objects to being asked what they are building toward. The same question, held well, opens the interior dimension without announcing it

---

## Why Founders Respond to This Entry Point

Founders are, almost by definition, people who started something from a vision of what could be. That forward orientation is often still the most alive part of them, even when they are exhausted and ground down by the operational demands of scale. Connecting them back to that original fire - or asking them to locate what version of it still burns - often creates energy in the conversation that the operational framing alone cannot.

The Luminous application:
- "What do you want this to become in ten years?"
- "What would it mean to you if this succeeded beyond your current imagination?"
- "What are you actually building - what's the thing underneath the business?"

These questions begin in the strategic register and move naturally toward meaning, identity, and legacy. For founders who would resist a direct invitation to explore their inner world, they represent a door that opens without announcing itself as a door.

---

## Connection to the Immunity to Change Approach

This entry point pairs naturally with the Immunity to Change methodology (see: [[Immunity to Change as a coaching tool at Scale]]). Once a leader has named what they want the organisation to become, the follow-up question - "what do you know you need to do differently to get there, and what keeps getting in the way?" - creates the structural conditions for surfacing hidden commitments and competing assumptions. Purpose provides the motivational charge; Immunity to Change provides the analytical structure.
