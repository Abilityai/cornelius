# Abdication dressed as empowerment - the leadership failure mode

## Core Insight

When leaders declare "you're all empowered, go forth" without providing direction, safety, or genuine authority, they have not empowered people - they have abandoned them. People feel lost, not free. This is abdication dressed as empowerment, and it is one of the most common failure modes at Threshold 2.

---

## Context and Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Widely recognised in leadership development and organisational psychology - consistent with research on leader behaviour, directive vs participative leadership, and psychological safety; Luminous has named and framed this with particular clarity
**Supporting Evidence**: Consistent with Heifetz's Adaptive Leadership distinction between mobilising people around hard problems vs. abdicating responsibility; consistent with the psychological safety research showing that clarity of expectations is prerequisite to safety

---

## The Anatomy of the Failure

Leaders who make this mistake are often well-intentioned. They have read about the shift from command-and-control to empowerment. They understand intellectually that the old way was limiting. They announce the change.

What they have not understood is that genuine empowerment requires three things they have not provided:

1. **Direction** - clarity about what people are empowered to do, within what parameters, toward what ends. Without this, "go forth" is chaos, not freedom.

2. **Safety** - the conditions under which it is genuinely safe to make decisions, fail, speak honestly, and bring problems forward. Without this, "you're empowered" is a trap - responsibility without protection.

3. **Genuine authority** - real decision-making power delegated in practice, not in principle. Without this, the empowerment is theatre. People test it once, find the limits, and retreat.

The result is a particular kind of organisational bewilderment - people feel responsible but not authorised, expected to perform freedom without being trusted with it.

---

## Why This Matters at Threshold 2

Leaders who want to move from command-and-control to an empowering style but have not understood what that actually requires of them personally are a core client profile at this threshold. The work is not helping them announce a new style - it is helping them understand what genuine transfer of authority means, and what it costs them personally to provide the direction, safety, and real authority that make empowerment real.
