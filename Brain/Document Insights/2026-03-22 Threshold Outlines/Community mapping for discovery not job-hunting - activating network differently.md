# Community Mapping for Discovery Not Job-Hunting - Activating Network Differently

## Core Insight

A specific methodology in the Renewal and Transition work: community mapping. The instruction is precise - activate their network not for job-hunting but for discovery. The distinction changes the nature of every conversation the leader has. Job-hunting asks "do you have something for me?" Discovery asks "help me think about what I'm becoming."

---

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - named explicitly in the Luminous methodology; consistent with narrative approaches to career transition and with Robert Kegan's constructive-developmental theory applied to professional networks

**Why the framing shift is significant**:

When leaders enter career transition, their instinct is often to activate their network transactionally - to find the next role. This mode has two problems:

1. It is premature. The leader has not yet done enough inner work to know what they are looking for. Transactional networking at this stage produces noise and sometimes bad matches.

2. It reduces the richness of their network to a job board. People in the network have context, wisdom, and perspective about the leader that is far more valuable than any open role they might know about.

**What discovery-oriented conversations look like**:

The leader approaches people not asking "what do you have?" but asking "what do you see?" They invite honest reflection from people who know them well. These conversations often surface strengths and patterns the leader cannot see from the inside. The network becomes a mirror and a thinking partner, not a resource to be exploited.

**Connection to the "try it on" methodology**: Community mapping often leads directly into experiential testing. Someone in the network may offer a board role or introduce the leader to a context where they can try something on. The discovery mode keeps these openings alive in a way that job-hunting mode would close down.
