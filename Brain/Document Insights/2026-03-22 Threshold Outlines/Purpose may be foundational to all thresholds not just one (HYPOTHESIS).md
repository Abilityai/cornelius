## Hypothesis

Purpose may not be merely one threshold among five - it may be the thread running through all of them. If true, this would mean that the Long-Term Impact threshold is not simply a domain of transformation but the foundational layer beneath every other threshold, with implications for how the entire Five Thresholds framework should be understood and sequenced.

The source document flags this directly: *"Worth discussing whether this threshold is foundational to the whole framework rather than just one of four."*

---

## Rationale

**Why This Might Be True**:

- At Threshold 1 (Scale): The leader who cannot hold growth is often one whose sense of purpose has not expanded with the organisation. The capacity question is also a meaning question.
- At Threshold 2 (Organisational Transformation): A leader cannot credibly ask their organisation to change if they have not asked the deeper "why" of what they are building and why it matters. Embodying the change requires a sense of purpose.
- At Threshold 3 (Succession): The leader who passes the baton is making a fundamentally purposive act - what am I leaving behind, and why does this particular continuity matter?
- At Threshold 4 (Long-Term Impact): Explicitly about purpose, legacy, stewardship.
- At Threshold 5 (Career Transition): The move from externally-defined to authentic identity is, at its heart, a move toward a life shaped by purpose rather than expectation.

If purpose underlies all five, the taxonomy is not a flat list of thresholds but a structure with one layer that grounds the others.

**Confidence Level**: Medium - the logic is coherent and the connections are plausible. But this is a conceptual argument, not an empirical one. The threshold framework may work precisely as a flat taxonomy for practical coaching reasons even if purpose is philosophically foundational.

---

## Testable Predictions

**If this hypothesis is correct, we would expect**:

1. Leaders working at any threshold who also have a clear sense of purpose would navigate their threshold more effectively than those who do not
2. Coaching at any threshold that incorporates a purpose inquiry (even briefly) would produce deeper shifts than coaching that focuses narrowly on the presenting threshold
3. Leaders who have resolved their purpose question would be more likely to seek Luminous support voluntarily, rather than being referred or triggered by crisis

**Falsification Criteria**:
- Evidence that leaders successfully navigate Scale, Transformation, or Succession thresholds with no engagement with purpose questions whatsoever
- Evidence that purpose inquiry at non-purpose thresholds is irrelevant or distracting

---

## Framework Implications (If True)

If purpose is foundational, the Five Thresholds might be better understood as:

- One **foundational layer** (Purpose/Dharma) that grounds all work
- Four **domain-specific thresholds** that are contextual expressions of the underlying purpose question

This would affect how Luminous presents the framework to clients, how the Mind maps threshold interactions, and potentially how coaching programmes are structured.
