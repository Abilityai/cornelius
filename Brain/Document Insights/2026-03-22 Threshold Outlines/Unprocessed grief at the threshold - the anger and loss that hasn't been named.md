# Unprocessed Grief at the Threshold - the Anger and Loss That Hasn't Been Named

## Core Insight

Leaders at the Metanoia threshold carry unprocessed grief - anger, loss, and sadness that has not yet been named. This is not abstract. The coaching work begins by creating the conditions for this material to surface. The transition cannot be designed from a clear mind until the emotional residue of what is ending has been acknowledged.

---

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Consistent with established grief psychology (Kübler-Ross, William Bridges' transitions framework) applied specifically to leadership career transition - a context where grief is rarely normalised or expected

**The naming matters**: "Grief" is the precise word. Not disappointment, not frustration, not stress. The ending of a chapter that has defined identity for decades is a genuine loss - of role, of community, of daily structure, of a clear sense of self-worth. High-achieving leaders are not trained to name this as grief, and so it often presents as irritability, restlessness, or a vague flatness that resists explanation.

**What the grief contains**:
- Loss of the role itself and what it provided (structure, status, community)
- Anger - often unacknowledged - about how the transition arrived (especially if involuntary)
- Sadness about the version of themselves that the role held
- Sometimes grief about the choices not made, the roads not taken

**Coaching implication**: The emotional processing is not preliminary work to be completed before the "real" coaching starts. It is the first phase of the work itself. The 10-12 session structure explicitly names "processing any residual emotions/grief" as the opening element, before values clarification, strengths work, or transition planning.

**Without naming the grief**: Leaders who skip this phase tend to make rushed decisions - immediately pursuing the next role, or intellectualising the transition as pure strategy. The grief resurfaces later, often in the new chapter, undermining it from within.
