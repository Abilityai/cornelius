## Core Framework

Next-generation family business leaders are consistently moving away from their parents' primary motivations - survival, building a name, financial security - toward something qualitatively different: long-term impact, stewardship, contribution to society, and in many cases, a spiritual dimension to their work. This is not a marginal shift in a few individuals; it appears as a generational pattern.

The document notes: "The potential in this group is remarkable." This is a practitioner observation about a cohort, not merely an individual aspiration.

---

## Context and Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - consistent with generational research on Millennials and Gen Z values, and with the broader literature on "post-material values" (Inglehart). Not yet empirically validated within family business succession specifically.
**Supporting Evidence**: Practitioner observation across succession engagements at Luminous
**Limitation**: Sample may be self-selecting - leaders who seek this kind of coaching may already be oriented toward meaning. Generalisation requires caution.

---

## The Generational Contrast

| Founding Generation | Next Generation |
|---|---|
| Survival and security | Stewardship and impact |
| Building a name | Leaving something meaningful |
| Financial success as primary measure | Contribution to society as measure |
| Pragmatic relationship to business | Sometimes spiritual relationship to purpose |

This divergence is not simply a values disagreement - it is a difference in the fundamental frame through which business is understood. It generates predictable friction: founders protecting what they built, heirs wanting to transform it.

---

## Implication for Succession Work

This shift is a resource, not a problem. Next-gen leaders motivated by impact and stewardship are capable of building something more enduring than what they inherited - if the transition is handled well. The risk is that this potential gets suppressed by obligation, founder resistance, or governance dysfunction before it can be activated.

The succession threshold, at its best, is where this generational transformation becomes possible.
