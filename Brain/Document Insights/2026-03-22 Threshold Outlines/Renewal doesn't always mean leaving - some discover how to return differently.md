# Renewal Doesn't Always Mean Leaving - Some Discover How to Return Differently

## Core Insight

One of the less obvious discoveries in the Renewal and Transition work: some leaders who arrive convinced they need to exit find, through the process, that what they actually need is to return to their current role differently - with clearer boundaries, renewed purpose, or simply more of themselves present. The threshold is the same; the destination varies.

---

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - a distinctive and counterintuitive observation from coaching practice that challenges the common assumption that this threshold is synonymous with departure

**Why this is significant**: Leaders often present the coaching need as a transition problem - "I need to work out what's next." But the framing assumes departure. The Luminous insight is that the threshold is a quality-of-presence question, not necessarily a location question. The discomfort is not always a signal to leave. Sometimes it is a signal to inhabit the current space more fully.

**What changes when someone returns differently**:
- Clearer personal boundaries - knowing what they will and will not carry
- Renewed sense of why the role matters - reconnecting to purpose rather than performance
- Less over-identification with the organisation - healthier relationship to title and status
- More of themselves visible to their team - less performance, more presence

**The reframing of the threshold**: "The transition is not always external. For some, the change required is within their current role. The threshold is reached whenever something has to shift; the question is what form that shift will take." This is a precise and important distinction. The Rewrite is a rewriting of identity. The physical location is secondary.

**Coaching implication**: Holding this possibility open from the beginning - not presuming departure - creates more honest coaching. The leader explores what they genuinely need rather than planning an exit they may not actually want.
