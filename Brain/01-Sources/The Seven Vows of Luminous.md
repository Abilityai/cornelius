# The Seven Vows of Luminous

*Taken by all who enter the inner circle of stewardship*

---

## The First Vow: The Vow of Service

I vow to place my work in service of wise conduct in those who hold great responsibility, so that decisions made today honour the dignity of all living beings and the wellbeing of generations yet unborn.

I will serve responsibility guided by conscience.

## The Second Vow: The Vow of Integrity

I vow to act with integrity even when it is costly, to choose what is right over what is expedient, and to protect the reputation and moral centre of Luminous above personal gain.

When faced with a choice between growth and truth, I vow to choose truth.

## The Third Vow: The Vow of Stewardship

I vow to hold Luminous as a trust.

I recognize that this institution existed before me and must endure beyond me.

I will act as a steward for a future I will not see, and I will release authority when my time has passed.

## The Fourth Vow: The Vow of Conscious Power

I vow to help leaders see power clearly: its weight, its consequences, and its moral reach.

I will guide those I serve to exercise leadership with wisdom, courage, and skilful compassion, always honouring their autonomy and agency.

## The Fifth Vow: The Vow of Practice

I vow to remain a student of the path, embracing lifelong learning.

I will continue my own inner work, training, and development, knowing that the depth of my presence sets the ceiling for the depth of my service.

I will strive to embody what I teach.

## The Sixth Vow: The Vow of Lineage

I vow to honour the wisdom traditions from which this work flows: Eastern and Western, ancient and emerging, spiritual and scientific.

I will protect the canon, rituals, and living knowledge of Luminous, updating methods where needed while preserving what must endure.

I will pass this knowledge forward intact.

## The Seventh Vow: The Vow of Continuity

I vow to protect the future of Luminous beyond my own tenure.

I will support succession, celebrate transition, and welcome renewal, including my own eventual transition to an elder.

I accept that leadership is temporary, and responsibility to the future is permanent.
