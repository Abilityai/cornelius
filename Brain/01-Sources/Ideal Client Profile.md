# Ideal Client Profile

*Archetype: The Leader Who Is Standing at a Threshold*

---

## Who They Are

- Senior leaders: founders, CEOs, next-generation family business leaders, long-tenured executives, or leaders navigating a career crossroads
- Typically in their late 30s to early 60s
- Have built something real (a business, a career, a reputation)
- Successful by every external measure that used to feel like enough
- Global (we work across geographies, industries, and cultures)
- What they share is not a job title - rather, they are at a threshold, a "moment that matters" for them

## What They Are Experiencing

### In the world around them:

- Living with increasing amounts of uncertainty and chaos, in their organisations, their industries, and the wider world
- Not sure how to navigate it; defaulting to what they can control, even when control is an illusion (like jumping out of a plane without a parachute, only to realise there is no ground)
- Dropping more balls than they used to; stretched in ways that feel unsustainable
- A creeping sense of hopelessness: that however hard they work, they can't change the world they're operating in

### In themselves:

- At a point where what has brought them here no longer fully works, and they sense it
- Exhausted in a way that sleep, exercise, and good intentions don't fix; on a treadmill they didn't consciously choose
- Anxious, though they may not name it that way. It shows up as irritability, over-functioning, an inability to switch off
- Trying to control more as a response to feeling less in control, which only deepens the exhaustion
- A growing flatness or loss of aliveness: going through the motions, successful but oddly flat
- A sense of incongruence: who they are inside no longer quite matches who they are being outside
- A persistent feeling that they are here for something more, without knowing what
- Carrying questions they don't feel they can ask anywhere else
- Searching outside themselves for the next thing that will make it feel meaningful, and never quite finding it

## What They May Not Be Saying Out Loud

- Despite being surrounded by people, they feel oddly alone
- There is a brave face that rarely comes down
- A fear that asking for help, or looking inward, signals weakness
- A sense of obligation: to the business, to the family, to those who depend on them
- Grief, sometimes, for the chapter that is ending, or the life that wasn't fully lived

## What They Are Ready For

- To ask different questions: not just "how do I perform better?" but "what am I actually here for?"
- To find a space where they don't have to pretend they have it all together
- To look inward, not just outward: to explore the patterns and ways of being that are shaping how they lead
- To move from reactive to reflective, to see more clearly before they act
- To be in the company of others who take these questions seriously
- To lead in a way that feels more like them

## What Might Give Them Pause

- This kind of work may feel unfamiliar - even uncomfortable
- They are wary of anything that feels soft or disconnected from the real pressures they face
- They are genuinely busy - prioritising their own development can feel indulgent
- Words like "spirituality" or "inner work" may create a moment of hesitation, even if the longing underneath is real

## How Luminous Helps

*See more clearly. Choose more wisely. Lead more consciously.*

- We accompany leaders on a path of becoming - weaving together ancient wisdom and modern science, working across body, mind, and spirit
- We work at the level of who the leader is becoming, not just what they are doing
