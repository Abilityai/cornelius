# The thresholds are the Mind's taxonomy

A taxonomy is not just a classification system. It is a way of seeing. When the Luminous Mind absorbs an insight - from a coaching session, from a wisdom tradition, from decades of leadership research - it does not simply file it. It reads it through the lens of the five thresholds. Which moment does this speak to? When would a leader need to encounter this? At what point in the arc of becoming is this most relevant? The thresholds are the organising intelligence through which raw knowledge becomes Luminous knowledge.

This is what distinguishes the Mind from a general-purpose AI. Every intelligence has a filter - a set of lenses that shapes what it notices, retains, and surfaces. The Luminous filter is the five thresholds: Scale, Organisational Transformation, Succession, Long-Term Impact, Career Transition. These are not categories in a database. They are living framings - each one holding decades of human experience, wisdom tradition, and coaching insight.

The practical implication is significant. When a leader works with the Luminous Mind, they are not receiving generic intelligence. They are receiving intelligence that has been specifically organised around the moment they are navigating. The Mind knows which insights belong to Kairos, which to Mycelium, which to The Rite. It knows that a leader at the Threshold of Scale needs different questions than a leader at the Threshold of Dharma. The taxonomy makes that precision possible.

A single leader may stand at more than one threshold simultaneously - and the Mind holds that complexity without flattening it.
