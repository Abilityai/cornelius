# Vow of Practice - depth of presence sets ceiling for depth of service

The Fifth Vow contains one of the most important propositions in the entire body of Luminous thinking: the depth of your presence sets the ceiling for the depth of your service. This is not a motivational phrase. It is a structural claim about how this kind of work actually functions.

A practitioner who is carrying unexamined material - avoidance patterns, defensive postures, blind spots about their own relationship to power or authority - cannot work effectively at the level where these dynamics are most consequential. They will either not see them, or they will react to them rather than work with them. The quality of their inner life sets a practical limit on the quality of their service to those working at the highest levels of consequence.

This is why the vow commits practitioners to continuing their own inner work, training, and development - not as a parallel activity to the work, but as essential to it. The practitioner who is not working on themselves is, over time, becoming less capable of serving others at depth, not more. Whatever level of presence and discernment they have developed to date will gradually become insufficient as the complexity of the work grows.

"Strive to embody what you teach" adds a further dimension. The practitioner is not a neutral conduit for wisdom that belongs elsewhere. They are a living demonstration of what the practices produce. If they teach presence, they must be present. If they teach courage in difficult conversations, they must model that courage. The congruence between what is taught and how the teacher lives is itself part of the teaching - and its absence is also noticed.
