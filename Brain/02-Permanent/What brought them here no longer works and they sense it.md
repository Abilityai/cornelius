# What brought them here no longer works and they sense it

Success has a particular way of becoming a trap. The strategies, habits, and ways of being that produced the results - the drive, the control, the relentless forward motion - worked. They worked so well that they became identity. And now, at a point of genuine inflection, those same strategies are beginning to constrain rather than serve. The leader knows it. Not always consciously, and rarely with the language to name it clearly. But they sense it.

The exhaustion is the first signal. Not ordinary tiredness - the kind that sleep and holidays reliably fix - but a deeper depletion that persists. It shows up as irritability under pressure, over-functioning in areas that should be delegated, an inability to truly switch off. It is the exhaustion of maintaining a way of operating that no longer fits the moment. The body often knows before the mind does.

The response is usually to try harder at what already worked: more control, more effort, more strategic planning. Like pulling harder on a door that opens the other way. The more a leader tightens their grip in response to feeling less in control, the deeper the exhaustion becomes. This is not a personal failing. It is a structural signal. The threshold is asking for something different, not something more.

Luminous names this experience directly and without drama, because the leaders who need to hear it are often carrying it in silence. The moment of recognition - when someone articulates what they have been quietly living - is often the beginning of genuine work. Something that has no name cannot be moved. Named, it becomes navigable.
