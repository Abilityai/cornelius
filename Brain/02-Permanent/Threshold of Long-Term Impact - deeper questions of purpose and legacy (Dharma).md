# Threshold of Long-Term Impact - deeper questions of purpose and legacy (Dharma)

There is a particular kind of leader who has, by any external measure, succeeded. The company grew. The deals closed. The reputation was built. The money arrived. And one morning - or perhaps gradually, over years - the realisation settles: this is no longer enough. Not because the work was wrong, but because a deeper question has begun to surface. What am I actually building this for? What will remain? What was this life in service of?

This is the Threshold of Long-Term Impact. Its ancient name is Dharma - the Sanskrit concept of one's sacred duty, the path that is uniquely and irreplaceably one's own. The working brand name is What Remains. Both names point toward the same invitation: to move from achievement to stewardship, from performance to purpose, from accumulating to contributing.

Leaders at this threshold are not in crisis. They are often at the height of their external success. But something is flat. The rewards that once felt motivating feel hollow. The goals that once organised their energy feel arbitrary. They are, as the Luminous client profile names it, searching outside themselves for the next thing that will make it feel meaningful - and never quite finding it. The next thing is always inward.

The work Luminous does at this threshold is some of the most significant: helping a leader clarify their Dharma - not what they think they should do with the second half, but what they are genuinely called toward. This requires real honesty, space for grief, and the courage to define success on entirely new terms.
