# The Seven Seals protect what Luminous is and always will be

Not everything in an institution should be open to revision. The capacity to adapt methods is a strength, but an institution that renegotiates its core purpose with each new generation of leadership has no core purpose - it only has preferences, which change. The Seven Seals are Luminous's answer to this problem: a defined set of principles that are structurally protected from revision and that define what Luminous is at its most essential.

The Seven Seals are: (1) Mission and Vision Permanence - the fundamental purpose endures regardless of how methods evolve; (2) Ownership Protection - Luminous cannot be sold or captured by external interests; (3) Spiritual Integrity - the contemplative foundation cannot be abandoned; (4) Impact Over Extraction - when forced to choose between profit and mission, mission wins; (5) Knowledge Preservation and Evolution - the canon is protected and transmitted across generations; (6) Succession as Sacred Obligation - leadership transitions must be planned and ritualized; (7) Service to All Life - the institution serves all living beings, not only those who can pay.

Together the Seals describe the non-negotiable character of Luminous. They are enforced by the Foundation's golden share, which gives the stewardship arm super-voting rights on any decision touching on these principles. This means that no founding partner, no powerful client, no external investor, and no future generation can fundamentally alter what Luminous is - only how it operates.

The existence of the Seals is itself a statement of values: that some things are more important than adaptability, and that real integrity requires knowing in advance which principles you will not compromise.
