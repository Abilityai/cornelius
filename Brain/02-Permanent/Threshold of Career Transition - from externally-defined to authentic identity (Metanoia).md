# Threshold of Career Transition - from externally-defined to authentic identity (Metanoia)

Some thresholds are chosen. Others arrive uninvited - through a restructure, a board decision, a health event, or the quiet accumulation of years spent in a role that no longer fits. Either way, the leader at a career crossroads is standing at the same essential question: who am I, now that I am no longer who I was?

This is the Threshold of Career Transition. Its ancient name is Metanoia - from the Greek, meaning a transformation of mind, a fundamental change of direction. The working brand name is The Rewrite. The language is precise: this is not an edit. It is a rewriting of identity - the release of an externally-defined self and the gradual discovery of one that is genuinely one's own.

For many leaders, identity has been profoundly tied to role. The title, the organisation, the responsibilities - these have provided structure, status, and a clear answer to the question "who are you?" When that structure falls away - whether chosen or not - what remains can feel disorienting. Some leaders rush immediately to the next role, seeking to fill the void. Others find themselves unexpectedly freed: released into a space where, for the first time, they can choose who they want to become.

Luminous holds this threshold with particular care, because it is the most personal. The work is not career strategy, though strategy may emerge. It is identity work - helping the leader grieve what is ending, discover what is true, and begin to imagine a next chapter that is written from the inside out rather than shaped by what others expect.
