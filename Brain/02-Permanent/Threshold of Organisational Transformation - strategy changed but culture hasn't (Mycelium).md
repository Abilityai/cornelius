# Threshold of Organisational Transformation - strategy changed but culture hasn't (Mycelium)

Strategy can change in a day. Culture takes years. This gap - between what the organisation says it is becoming and how it actually behaves - is one of the most common and costly failures in leadership. Leaders announce transformations. They redesign processes, hire new people, commission new strategies. And then they wonder why nothing feels different. The answer is almost always the same: the culture has not caught up, because the leader has not gone first.

This is the Threshold of Organisational Transformation. Its ancient name is Mycelium - the underground fungal network that connects the roots of an entire forest, invisible and essential. The working brand name is Going First. The mycelium image carries the truth: real transformation happens beneath the surface, in the invisible network of relationships, habits, assumptions, and ways of being that actually govern how an organisation operates day to day.

The Luminous understanding of this threshold is precise: the leader cannot ask of others what they have not asked of themselves. If the transformation requires more openness, more trust, more willingness to sit with uncertainty - then the leader must embody those qualities first. Not perform them. Embody them. People in organisations are exquisitely sensitive to the difference. They watch what their leaders do under pressure, not what they say in all-hands meetings.

The work here is genuine inner transformation, not change management technique. The leader who goes first - who does the real work of becoming the change they are asking for - changes the energetic conditions of the whole organisation. Culture is downstream of character.
