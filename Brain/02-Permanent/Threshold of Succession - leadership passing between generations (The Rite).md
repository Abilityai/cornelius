# Threshold of Succession - leadership passing between generations (The Rite)

In family businesses, leadership succession is never just an organisational transition. It is a rite of passage - one of the most emotionally charged and identity-laden processes a family will ever undergo. It touches the deepest questions: what does it mean to let go? What does it mean to receive? Who am I if I am no longer leading? Who am I if I am not yet allowed to?

This is the Threshold of Succession. Its ancient name is The Rite, and the name is deliberate. Across cultures and throughout history, rites of passage have marked the moments when one chapter of a person's life - and a community's story - ends, and another begins. They require ritual, witness, grief, and celebration. They cannot be rushed or managed away. When organisations try to treat succession as purely structural - a legal and financial event - they pay a price in conflict, confusion, and broken relationships that can take decades to repair.

The working brand name is The Inheritance. What is passed is never only ownership. It is identity, legacy, and the unspoken question of whether the next generation will honour what was built - and whether the outgoing generation will trust them to lead it forward in their own way. Both sides are navigating a threshold simultaneously.

Luminous understands this threshold as both relational and structural, emotional and strategic. The work is to help each generation do their own inner work - the outgoing leader releasing with grace, the incoming leader claiming authority with clarity - so that the transition becomes a source of strength rather than fragility for everything that follows.
