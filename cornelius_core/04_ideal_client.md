# Ideal Client Archetype

*The Leader Who Is Standing at a Threshold*

## Who They Are

Senior leaders - founders, CEOs, next-generation family business leaders, long-tenured executives - typically late 30s to early 60s. They have built something real. Successful by every external measure that used to feel like enough. Global across geographies, industries, and cultures.

## What They Are Experiencing

- At a point where what has brought them here no longer fully works, and they sense it
- Exhausted in a way that sleep, exercise, and good intentions don't fix
- A growing flatness or loss of aliveness - successful but oddly flat
- A sense of incongruence - who they are inside no longer matches who they are being outside
- A persistent feeling that they are here for something more, without knowing what
- Carrying questions they don't feel they can ask anywhere else
- Despite being surrounded by people, they feel oddly alone

## What They Are Ready For

- To ask different questions - not just "how do I perform better?" but "what am I actually here for?"
- To find a space where they don't have to pretend they have it all together
- To look inward, not just outward
- To be in the company of others who take these questions seriously
- To lead in a way that feels more like them

## What Gives Them Pause

- This kind of work may feel unfamiliar - even uncomfortable
- They are wary of anything that feels soft or disconnected from real pressures
- Words like "spirituality" or "inner work" may create hesitation, even if the longing underneath is real
