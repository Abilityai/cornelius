## 1. Description of this threshold

- The shift from short-term to long-term thinking - from "what do I want now?" to "what impact do I want to have on the world beyond my own lifetime?"
- In developmental terms: a move from I to we, from ego to contribution, from achievement to stewardship
- Can arrive as a quiet question ("is this all there is?") or as a sudden jolt (illness, milestone, selling a business, retirement)
- Not age-dependent - can emerge at 40 or 65; but tends to surface once basic needs (security, belonging, achievement) have been met
- Three related concepts that belong together here, as different entry points to the same underlying shift:
  - Purpose (why am I here?)
  - Legacy (what is the broader impact I want to have?)
  - Stewardship (what am I custodian of, for future generations?)

## 2. Typical client profile

- Senior leaders, founders, or family business owners (especially next-generation business owners) who have achieved significant external success and find it is no longer enough
- Often in their 40s-60s, though the threshold is not age-gated
- May have recently sold a business, retired, or hit a financial milestone - and found the relief shorter-lived than expected

## 3. Common signals and challenges

- Loss of aliveness - going through the motions, successful but flat
- Always chasing more (money, power, status) without being able to say why or what "enough" looks like
- Telling the same story on repeat; comparing themselves to others in similar positions
- The "island" pattern: "once I have X, then I'll focus on what matters" - but X keeps moving
- A vague but persistent sense of "I'm here for something more" - without knowing what
- Inertia: knowing something needs to shift but not knowing how to start
- For family business leaders specifically: the weight of obligation ("I was always supposed to take this over") alongside uncertainty about whether they actually want it

## 4. The opportunity for growth

- **For the individual:** discovering that their deepest motivation is not more of what they already have - and finding the specific form their contribution wants to take
- **For teams:** moving from "we're going to be the best at X" (no one's really inspired) to a collectively-held sense of the longer impact the organisation wants to have - which is intrinsically motivating
- The relief that comes when someone finally connects with a sense of longer-term / deeper meaning

## 5. How we work with leaders and teams at this threshold

### 1:1 Coaching

- Clearing the ground first: Purpose cannot be heard until the ego-noise is quieted. The work often begins with clearing emotional blocks, old stories, reactive patterns - until the client knows what it feels like to hear the voice of their soul
- Purpose exercise: Past-present-future reflection - what has always been true about you? What's true now? What is your highest wisdom saying? A purpose emerges from them, rather than being imposed
- The "precious human life" question: Used as a recurring entry point - not asked once, but returned to over time to track shifts
- Expanding the definition of success: Moving from "what do I want?" to "what is the world asking for from me / from us?"
- Small doses to overcome inertia: Not waiting for the grand gesture - what's one thing you can do this week that is in the direction of this? Let it compound

### Team Coaching

- Team / systemic dimension: With leadership teams, the question becomes: what is the world asking for from us collectively? This reframe - from inside-out to outside-in - changes the conversation entirely
- Retreats and immersives: A 1-2 day personal team retreat as a container for deep reflection; or an immersive where leaders encounter the end impact of their work directly (e.g. the GSK Kenya example - meeting the people your products actually serve)

### Cohort offering

A peer group for leaders at this threshold to share experiences, challenge each other's thinking, and build accountability around their long-term intentions.

## 6. Additional reflections about this threshold

- **Purpose wraps around everything:** Purpose isn't just one threshold - it's the thread running through all of them. Worth discussing whether this threshold is foundational to the whole framework rather than just one of four
- **The relief is visceral:** When clients finally touch this place, it's physically felt. This isn't intellectual work
- **"Legacy" is a problematic word:** Feels masculine, old, tied to plaques and buildings. "Long-term impact," "stewardship," or even "what you're leaving behind" land better across genders and cultures. Worth being intentional about language
- **Not just for those winding down:** This threshold is live for a 40-year-old founder as much as a 65-year-old retiring CEO. The framing shouldn't skew too old or it will feel irrelevant to a large part of the audience
