## 1. Description of this threshold

This threshold arises when a person is at a crossroads in their career: either triggered by:
- An external event (redundancy, leadership re-shuffle)
- Life milestone (e.g. kids through college - first time they can ask "what do I want?")
- Burnout and/or a desire for renewal, to transition to work they find more meaningful

Beneath this is often a sense that they have been living someone else's life; that they are ready to move from an externally-defined career identity to a self-authored one.

The transition is not always external. For some, the change required is within their current role - finding a way to lead, engage or show up differently without leaving. The threshold is reached whenever something has to shift; the question is what form that shift will take.

## 2. Typical client profile

- Late 40s to late 50s, 20-30+ years of career, often at one or two organisations
- Has reached the top of their field - C-suite, partner, regional/global leader
- Financially secure enough to take time with the transition (aligns with our price point)
- Often the primary family provider - with significant identity and pressure attached to that
- May be burned out, in survival mode, or feeling bored or flat in a role they are still good at, well-respected, but no longer energised by

## 3. Common signs / challenges at this threshold

- Loss of aliveness / joy - going through the motions with a growing flatness
- Incongruence - 'who I am inside no longer matches who I'm being outside'
- Survival mode - functioning, delivering, but running on empty with no sense of why
- The "I've done it all" feeling - not burnout exactly, but a quiet sense of completion; nothing left to prove, but no clear sense of what's next
- Restlessness despite external success - getting approached for other roles, respected by peers, but privately wondering if there's more
- Over-identification with the org - can't separate self from company or title
- For men especially: deep conditioning around being the provider - when that feels threatened, significant internal crumbling
- Unable to name their own strengths beyond the role
- Unprocessed grief - anger, loss and sadness that hasn't been named yet

## 4. The opportunity for growth

- Reclaim authorship of their own life - write their own story for the first time (pathway to authenticity)
- Rediscover strengths, values and purpose that were always there, hidden beneath the role
- Build a new, broader definition of success - beyond status and income
- Recover a sense of aliveness: energy, curiosity, presence
- Ripple effect: families and communities benefit when leaders make this transition well

Note: this is primarily an individual threshold - team/org dimension is less central here than at others.

## 5. How we work with leaders at this threshold

### 1:1 Coaching Package

A 10-12 session coaching package designed to support them through whatever form their renewal or transition takes - whether that is a move to something entirely new, or finding a different way to inhabit what they already have, which includes:
- Processing any residual emotions / grief about the transition threshold
- Identifying what else it's time to let go of (old identities, habits, ways of being)
- Establishing a new inner compass (values, strengths, purpose, non-negotiables) - including Mastery Exercise (naming five things you're genuinely good at); Values Map; Purpose exercise
- Re-defining what success looks like (including, as needed, how much they actually need to earn - for most people it's much lower than they expect)
- Community mapping - activate their network not for job-hunting but for discovery
- 'Try it on' - safe test environments (volunteering, board roles, month at a vineyard) to make the next step real rather than theoretical

## 6. Additional reflections about this threshold

- **Timing:** Ultimately, this is an 18-month to 2-year journey; sessions will be intense at times, quiet at others - we hold this with patience. Sometimes clients take a break for a few months while they work through options - this is part of the process.
- **Support:** Fear of letting down loved ones is often the real blocker - but spouses and families are typically much more supportive than the coachee expects.
- **Specificity:** Almost nobody has done the actual financial maths - even finance professionals.
- **Renewal doesn't always mean leaving:** One of the less obvious discoveries in this work is that some clients who arrive convinced they need to exit find, through the process, that what they actually need is to return to their current role differently - with clearer boundaries, renewed purpose, or simply more of themselves present. The threshold is the same; the destination varies.
