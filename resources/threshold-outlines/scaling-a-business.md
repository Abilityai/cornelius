## 1. Description of this threshold

- Arises when a business is growing faster than the leader's capacity to hold it, and the way they have always led begins to actively constrain the organisation
- At its heart, it is a meditation on letting go: of control, of doing, of being the one who holds everything together
- The leader must make a fundamental shift from lead dancer to choreographer; from the person taking the hill to the general who stands back and lets field commanders lead
- This is not primarily a structural or operational challenge. The org chart can be redrawn, but if the leader's inner world hasn't shifted, the bottleneck simply relocates

## 2. Typical client profile

- Founders or long-tenure CEOs, often 35-55, who built something from nothing and whose identity is deeply entwined with the business
- Senior leaders in larger organisations who have absorbed additional scope and are struggling to lead differently at the new scale
- Highly driven, hands-on by nature - used to being the engine of growth and the decision-maker of last resort
- May not yet recognise themselves as the bottleneck; more likely to frame the problem as a team or talent issue
- Typically not yet doing deep inner work - their readiness for that will vary and needs to be met where they are

## 3. Common signals and challenges

- Energy drain across the team - people feel underutilised or disempowered; the leader is doing work that isn't theirs to do
- The revolving door - COOs, heads of sales, etc. brought in as saviours and dismissed within months; the pattern repeats without the leader seeing their own role in it
- Paternalistic decision-making - everything still flows through the founder or CEO; the team is waiting to be told rather than leading
- Performance stalling despite effort - the harder the leader works, the less traction the organisation gains
- Frantic energy and growing lack of clarity - busyness masking the absence of real strategic leadership
- Misalignment in the executive team - people pulling in different directions because authority hasn't been genuinely redistributed
- The 'all hands on deck' to 'we need real structure' moment - the scrappy, collaborative startup culture that worked at one size is actively breaking things at the next
- Over-identification with the business - the founder can't distinguish between what's best for the company and what feels safe for them personally

## 4. The opportunity for growth

- **For the leader:** the chance to evolve from operator to architect - to become a true leader of leaders, leading at the level of vision, culture and system rather than task and decision. Letting go of control is not a loss - it is the condition for both the business and the leader to reach their next level
- **For the team:** When the leader genuinely redistributes authority, the executive team steps up - often revealing capability that was always there but had no room to emerge
- **For the organisation:** Scale handled well builds resilience and longevity; mishandled, it leads to stalling growth, talent attrition, and eventual crisis

## 5. How we work with leaders and teams at this threshold

### 1:1 Coaching with the leader

- Hold up the mirror first: Interview the executive team (and sometimes wider organisation) to surface honest feedback - framed not as critique but as: what is the potential here, and what will it take to reach it?
- Redesign the role, repeatedly: As the business scales, the CEO/founder role needs active redesign every few months - what decisions should they be making? What should be shared or delegated entirely?
- Inner work, introduced with care: The reasons leaders struggle to let go are rarely about skill - they are about identity, fear, and often something deeper (a need to prove, a fear of irrelevance). We introduce this through trust and at the pace the client is ready for
- Purpose as a way in: Connecting the leader to the longer-term vision - what do they want this to become? - can be a less threatening opening for deeper reflection
- Immunity to change approach: Helping the leader name what they know they need to do differently, then excavating the hidden commitments and assumptions that keep them doing the opposite

### Team coaching / offsite

- Once 1:1 trust is established, move to the team: what does this leadership group need to become to match the complexity ahead?
- Create shared agreements about how authority is distributed and decisions are made
- Build the psychological safety for honest feedback to flow - upward, downward and sideways
- A rhythm of regular reflection (e.g. monthly small-group sessions using a structured self-examination methodology) so that self-inquiry becomes a habit rather than a crisis response

## 6. Additional reflections about this threshold

- **The bottleneck is rarely who they think it is:** Leaders here almost always externalise the problem first - it's a talent issue, a market issue, a team issue. One of the most important moments is when they begin to see their own place in the system
- **Readiness for inner work varies significantly:** Unlike leaders at the career transition or long-term impact thresholds, founders at scale are often earlier in their development journey. The ratio of outer to inner work may start at 80/20 and shift gradually - pushing too hard too fast loses them
- **The vulnerability beneath the strength:** Founders present as strong and self-sufficient, and often feel completely alone. When they finally trust enough to share fears - about the business, their family, their identity - it is one of the most moving moments in coaching. Working with Luminous should feel like an exhale
- **Thresholds overlap:** Leaders at scale are often simultaneously at another threshold - long-term impact in particular frequently surfaces once the immediate pressure eases. Be alert to this.
