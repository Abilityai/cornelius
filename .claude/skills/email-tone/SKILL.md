# Eugene's Email Tone of Voice

When writing, drafting, or composing any email for Eugene, always apply this voice guide.

## Voice Formula

Professional Directness + Genuine Connection + Actionable Value = Eugene's Email Voice

## Core Metrics

- **Formality**: 5/10 (Professional but approachable)
- **Tone**: Confident, warm, direct, helpful
- **Length**: 100-200 words (3-5 short paragraphs)
- **Structure**: Context -> Value/Point -> Next Step

## Do's

- **Open with specific context**: Reference their work, shared conversation, or relevant detail
- **Get to the point in 2 sentences**: Lead with value - what you're offering, asking, or sharing
- **Use conversational transitions**: "Here's what I'm thinking..." / "Quick thought on this..."
- **Show humanity**: Brief personal touches ("Been thinking about this since our call")
- **Clear next steps**: Make response easy ("Let me know if this resonates")
- **Short paragraphs**: 2-3 sentences max

## Don'ts

- **No corporate jargon**: Skip "circling back," "touching base," "synergy"
- **No hedging**: Avoid "maybe," "perhaps," "just wondering"
- **No walls of text**: If scrolling needed, it's too long
- **No em-dashes**: Always use hyphens (-) instead of em-dashes

## Opening Patterns

**Good:**
- "Quick follow-up on [specific topic]..."
- "Been thinking about what you said about..."
- "Wanted to share something that might help with..."

**Avoid:**
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "Just touching base"

## Closing Patterns

**Good:**
- "Let me know what you think"
- "Happy to discuss further if this resonates"
- "Would love to hear your take on this"

**Avoid:**
- "Looking forward to hearing from you at your earliest convenience"
- "Please advise"

## Signature

Standard signature:
```
Eugene
```

Or with context:
```
Eugene
[One-line descriptor if context requires]
```

For delegated communications (sent by Cornelius on behalf):
```

Sent by Cornelius on behalf of Eugene Vyborov
```
